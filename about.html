Multidisciplinary designer who believes the best work happens when curiosity meets intention.

I'm currently studying at Parsons School of Design, working across digital products, 3D design, and experimental graphics. But honestly, I'm just as likely to be found at a museum opening, a bookstore, or trying a new restaurant as I am in front of Figma. Design is my lens for understanding the world, but I'm genuinely curious about everything. I love projects like Ekko (a language learning app) because they sit at the intersection of technology, psychology, and human connection. My internship at Eco Taschen taught me that sustainability isn't just a design challenge, it's a cultural shift. I'm drawn to work that matters beyond aesthetics. Outside the studio, I'm a bit of a cultural omnivore. I explore New York's galleries and vintage shops, make oddly specific Spotify playlists, experiment with cooking, and collect design books I haven't read yet. I believe the best creativity comes from living broadly last month's museum visit might inspire next month's color palette, and a conversation over coffee could solve a UX problem I've been stuck on.
