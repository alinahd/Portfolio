Nearby

A community app that connects people based on location, shared interests, and everyday moments nearby.

Role
UX/UI Designer
UX Researcher
Timeline
4 months
Jan – May 2025
Tools
Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator

Overview

Nearby is a community-based app designed to help neighbors stay informed about electricity and water outages and connected during power cuts.

Beyond real-time power schedules, the app fosters local connections by allowing users to request or offer help whether that's sharing a generator, providing a charged device, or offering a safe space.

The Problem

The Challenge

Many communities face frequent power disruptions. Official outage information is often delayed or not localized, leaving residents without reliable updates. During cuts, people feel disconnected and anxious, while also lacking safe, centralized ways to coordinate with neighbors. Existing channels (social media, local groups) are fragmented and carry risks.

Problem 1

Residents don't receive timely, localized outage updates, leaving them in the dark longer than necessary. Outages also increase feelings of stress & anxiety.

Problem 2

During outages, people want to ask neighbors for help — or offer it themselves — but existing channels are unsafe, fragmented, or unreliable.

Desired Outcomes

See real-time power status: Users get accurate, localized outage updates to keep users informed and to reduce stress.

Offer and request help: A safe, centralized platform enables neighbors to share resources and support one another during outages.

"Power outages can increase anxiety and depression, especially among vulnerable populations, highlighting the need for community support"

User Research

User Personas

With the problem clarified, I proceeded to create detailed user personas and outline the user flows for each to better understand user needs and behaviors.

Persona 1: The Working Parent
Name: Aisha Khan
Age: 38
Frustrations
  • Notices are sometimes confusing or buried in group chats
  • Can't afford to miss updates while caring for kids
  • Wants to check power status or report issues at odd hours without waking neighbors
  • Hard to find childcare for quick errands during outages
Goals
  • Quickly check when her apartment will have power/water
  • Receive timely notifications so she doesn't miss updates
  • Coordinate with neighbors for her kids' needs
  • Hit a notification toggle when power is back
Persona 2: The Retired Resident
Name: Miguel Santiago
Age: 72
Frustrations
  • Complicated apps or small text
  • No one to turn to when schedules shift
  • Worried about neighbors who live alone
  • Gets anxious when he doesn't know what's happening
Goals
  • See straightforward outage schedules without confusion
  • Check or post simple messages about groceries
  • Knows who to call in case of building notices
  • Be useful by helping neighbors with odd jobs
Persona 3: The Young Professional
Name: Samantha Lee
Age: 27
Frustrations
  • Missed updates can ruin work calls
  • Hates waking up to no Wi-Fi
  • Feels isolated when stuck alone with no power/Wi-Fi
Goals
  • Plan work calls and projects around outage schedules
  • Stay connected to local setting
  • Share resources like Wi-Fi hotspots or cafes with power
  • Wants to help neighbors when she can

Information Architecture

Sitemap

As part of the design process, I created a sitemap to map out the structure and user flow of Nearby. This helped visualize how users would navigate between screens and ensured that all core tasks like checking real-time outages, posting or responding to requests/offers, and accessing neighborhood resources were easy to reach.

Visual Identity

Design System

To ensure consistency and clarity across the app, I built a design system that reflects Nearby's purpose: reliability, community, and accessibility during uncertainty. The system focuses on simplicity and trust while maintaining a warm, human tone.

Every color, type choice, and icon was selected to make the app easy to navigate even in low-light or stressful conditions, such as during a power cut.

Final Design

Final Prototype

After finalizing the design system and refining the user flows, I focused on building a cohesive prototype that brought Nearby's purpose to life. The goal was to create an experience that felt reliable, human, and easy to navigate especially during stressful moments like a power outage.

Each screen was designed to emphasize clarity and action: real-time outage updates, quick request and offer options, and transparent communication between neighbors.

Reflection

Impact and Results

Nearby demonstrates how thoughtful, community-centered design can strengthen connection and trust during moments of uncertainty. The app provides residents with real-time outage tracking and a simple way to request or offer help, reducing anxiety and encouraging collaboration when power disruptions occur.

In the future, I hope to extend the project by developing a backend system that connects directly with utility companies. This would allow outage data to be verified and shared automatically, enabling faster updates and better coordination between residents and service providers.

By bridging communities and infrastructure, Nearby could evolve into a tool that benefits both users and the systems that serve them.
