Ekko

An interactive storytelling app helping children practice speech sounds through play, designed for parents and children to use together.

Children with Speech Sound Disorders benefit most from early, consistent intervention but current tools fail to support parents in sustaining effective at-home practice.

The Problem

The Challenge

Children with Speech Sound Disorders (SSD), particularly phonological disorders, benefit most from early and consistent intervention. The PACT (Parents and Children Together) model emphasizes collaborative family involvement in therapy, but current tools and resources fail to support parents in understanding, sustaining, and tracking at-home practice.

Opportunity

How Might We

How might we help parents and children work together to engage in structured, interactive, and accessible home-based speech therapy?

Solution

Value Proposition

Making speech practice playful, simple, and purposeful. By combining evidence-based speech strategies with interactive storytelling, Ekko helps children practice speech sounds through short stories and play-based exercises.

Prioritization

Feature Ranking

I conducted feature ranking to validate which features would deliver the most value to users and inform prioritization decisions for the product roadmap. This method helped bridge the gap between my design assumptions and actual user needs, revealing that progress tracking and feedback systems were critical priorities for parents and experts in the field.

Insights

Key Learnings

Gratification Drives Engagement

Participants emphasized the need for rewards, badges, and recognition.

Children learn best when they feel celebrated—visible progress and rewards act as motivators.

Design implication: A rewards page that visually marks progress and builds motivation.

Control Matters

Participants wanted manual control to flip back and forth between pages in the story flow instead of it being automatic.

Homepage layout also felt cluttered and should be updated to feel more intuitive for children.

Design implication: Integrate clear back or next buttons.

Clarity Over Numbers

Participants found the percentage scores unclear and unhelpful. They didn't know what 5% or 10% represented.

Design implication: Replace % with clear progress indicators such as "7/10 sounds mastered" or "3 new words learned today," plus badges or stars for achievements.

Brand Strategy

Brand Positioning

To differentiate Ekko in the competitive landscape of speech therapy tools, I positioned the brand at the intersection of education and play. The positioning strategy emphasizes accessibility, evidence-based methods, and family-centered learning while maintaining a warm, approachable tone that resonates with both parents seeking effective solutions and children who want engaging experiences.
